Latitude's Affect on Temperature

From the humidity plot, it seems that globally the air is relatively humid (>60% humidity) and there are few places with a % humidity less than 40%. We see a sort of bimodal distrbution of the latitudes of such low-moisture cities - they appear to be centered around +/- 20 degrees above the equator, which corresponds to where much of the Earth's hot deserts lie (e.g. Sahara desert, American Southwest).

That notwithstanding, we don't see a real correlation between latitude and percent humidity. This implies that humidity may be dominated by other factors, like proximity to bodies of water and jet stream patterns.
